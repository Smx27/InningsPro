# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the InningsPro community and its projects will be treated with respect and dignity.

As contributors and maintainers of this project, we are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** — Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being collaborative** — Share knowledge and help others learn
- **Being professional** — Focus on what is best for the community
- **Being accountable** — Accept criticism gracefully and apologize when appropriate
- **Assuming good intent** — Interpret actions positively unless clear evidence suggests otherwise
- **Listening actively** — Seek first to understand, then to be understood
- **Celebrating diversity** — Value diverse perspectives and experiences

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** — Any form of offensive comments or unwelcome attention
- **Discrimination** — Based on personal characteristics or group membership
- **Intimidation** — Threats, bullying, or coercion
- **Trolling** — Deliberately posting inflammatory content to provoke reactions
- **Spam** — Unsolicited advertising or repetitive off-topic messages
- **Sexual content** — Unwanted sexual advances, jokes, or imagery
- **Doxxing** — Sharing private information without consent
- **Abuse of power** — Misusing moderator or maintainer privileges

### Professional Disagreement

Technical disagreements are healthy and encouraged. However:

- Focus on the **idea, not the person**
- Provide **evidence and reasoning** for your position
- **Listen to counterarguments** with an open mind
- Be willing to **change your mind** if convinced
- Disagree **respectfully and professionally**

---

## Our Responsibilities

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

- **Clarifying standards** of acceptable behavior
- **Enforcement** of this code of conduct
- **Moderation** of discussions and contributions
- **Creating a safe space** for all participants

### Expected Actions

Maintainers will:

- Address violations promptly and fairly
- Keep reporter information confidential
- Explain decisions and reasoning clearly
- Appeal processes will be available for serious actions

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Official project events and conferences
- Project-related communications (email, Slack, Discord, etc.)
- Unofficial events representing the project

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct:

1. **Contact maintainers** via email: See repository for contact details
2. **Provide details:**
   - What happened?
   - Who was involved?
   - When did it occur?
   - Are there witnesses?
   - Any supporting evidence or links?

3. **Confidentiality** — Reports will be handled confidentially
4. **No retaliation** — We protect reporters from reprisal

### Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledge** receipt within 48 hours
2. **Investigate** thoroughly and fairly
3. **Consult** with all parties (if appropriate)
4. **Decide** on appropriate action
5. **Communicate** outcome to reporter and violator

### Enforcement Actions

Violations may result in:

- **Warning** — Verbal or written reminder of standards
- **Temporary restriction** — Temporary ban from community spaces
- **Permanent ban** — Removal from project and community

Severity and context determine the response. Serious first violations (harassment, threats) may result in immediate bans.

## Support Resources

### Getting Help

If you're struggling with something:

- **Reach out to maintainers** — We're here to help
- **Ask questions** in discussions or issues
- **Seek mentorship** from experienced contributors
- **Take breaks** — Community participation should be sustainable

### Mental Health

Contributing should be enjoyable. If you're experiencing:

- Burnout — Talk to maintainers about scaling back
- Conflict — We can help mediate respectfully
- Discrimination — We have zero tolerance
- Other concerns — We're here to listen
